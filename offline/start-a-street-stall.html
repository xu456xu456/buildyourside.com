🛍️ Start a Street Stall (Australia Example)

Use this as a quick guide — it’s based on Australia’s weekend markets, but works almost anywhere with small changes.

💡 Why Try It

Weekend and community markets are great for beginners. You don’t need a shop, just a small table and some effort. Low cost, real cash, and friendly vibes every weekend.

📍 Where to Sell

  • 🗓️ Local weekend markets
  • 🎪 Community fairs or pop-ups
  • 🎄 Holiday events or night markets

Search “your city + weekend market stall” to find spots nearby.

🧺 What to Sell

  • 🧦 Socks, caps, or small fashion items
  • 💅 Nail kits or handmade crafts
  • 🍪 Snacks or drinks (check food safety rules)
  • 🪴 Candles, stickers, or plants

Start with something you enjoy — your energy helps sales.

📋 Basic Rules

  • 📄 Most markets need a stall permit ($20–$80/day)
  • 🧾 Insurance is often required
  • 🍴 Food sellers need safety registration
  • ⚡ Power use may need tested cables

Check your local city website for exact details.

💰 Costs & Profit

Example:

  • Buy 60 pairs of socks @ $2 = $120
  • Sell for $6 each → $225 revenue
  • Minus stall & fees ($50) = ~$55 profit in one weekend

Start small, learn what sells, then repeat.

⚙️ Supplies

Buy from online stores or local wholesalers. Test small orders first. Avoid fake or branded goods.

📑 Tax & Tips

Market income counts as taxable. Keep notes of what you spend and earn. If you make it a real business, get an ABN in Australia (or your local ID).

🌍 Other Countries

This idea works everywhere — just follow your local rules. Some cities call it “street vending,” some “pop-up shops.” Adjust prices and products to what people buy most in your area.
