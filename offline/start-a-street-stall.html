🛍️ Start a Street Stall (Australia Example)

Weekend Market / Community Market playbook you can adapt to your own country or city.

Scope: This guide uses Australia as the main example (Weekend & Community Markets). Rules, permits, and costs vary by country and council — always check your local requirements.

💡 1️⃣ Why Markets Work

Weekend and community markets in Australia are friendly to small sellers — predictable foot traffic, low startup cost, and supportive vibes. If you stay consistent and legal, a simple stall can bring steady weekend income.

📍 2️⃣ Where to Sell

  • Weekend Markets: Found in almost every city and suburb — book ahead.
  • Community Events: School fairs, charity days, local festivals.
  • Pop-up Markets: Seasonal events (Christmas, night markets, spring fairs).

Tip: Search “<your city> weekend market stall booking” or check your local council’s market directory.

🧺 3️⃣ What to Sell

  • 🧦 Socks & simple accessories — cheap, easy, always needed.
  • 💅 Nail art / handmade sets — small, creative, and profitable.
  • 🍪 Snacks / small bites — good margin if you meet food safety rules.
  • 🪴 Candles, crafts, plants, stickers — light and easy to stock.

Start with something you enjoy selling — enthusiasm sells best.

📋 4️⃣ Permits & Basic Rules (Australia)

  • 📄 Stall Permit: Required for most markets (AUD $20–$80/day).
  • 🧾 Public Liability Insurance: Needed at many venues.
  • 🍴 Food Handling: Register if selling edible products (differs by state).
  • ⚡ Electrical Safety: Get cords “tested & tagged” if using power.
  • 🏷️ Clear signage & pricing: Always display honest info.

Outside Australia — replace these with your country’s street vendor or business license rules.

💰 5️⃣ Costs & Profit Example

  • 🪙 Stall Fee: $20–$80/day
  • 🛡️ Insurance: $10–$30/day
  • 🛍️ Display & Bags: $20–$60
  • 📱 Payment Device: $0–$60 (Square, PayID QR)
  • 📦 Initial Stock: $50–$300

Example: Buy 60 pairs of socks @ $2 each ($120 total). Sell 45 pairs for $225 → minus stall/insurance $50 = ≈ $55 profit (one weekend).

⚙️ Sourcing Inventory

  • 🌐 Online sites / wholesalers: Test small orders first (quality control).
  • 🏬 Local suppliers: Ideal for consistent restock.
  • 🎨 Handmade items: Higher margin, more creativity, more time.

📑 Tax & Compliance (Australia)

  • 💵 Report your income properly — market sales are taxable.
  • 🔢 Get an ABN if operating as a small business.
  • 💼 GST registration only if annual turnover exceeds the threshold.

🌍 Adapt for Other Countries

Markets exist everywhere. Whether you’re in Asia, Europe, or North America, adapt based on your city’s reality:

  • 📜 Regulations: Use your city’s vendor or event permit system.
  • 🛒 Products: Local favorites sell better — food, art, phone cases, small gifts.
  • 💱 Pricing: Adjust for your currency and market demand.
  • 🏦 Tax: Follow your country’s freelancer or small business tax rules.
  • 🚚 Suppliers: Local e-commerce (Shopee, Lazada, Amazon, etc.) works fine.

Each country’s management, fees, and profits differ — use this guide as a flexible template.
