How to Start a Street Stall (Offline Side Hustle)

Low cost, flexible hours. Always check local regulations before selling.

1) What to Sell

  • Snacks/drinks, handmade crafts, phone accessories, stickers, postcards.
  • Start with 1–2 products to test demand before expanding.

2) Location & Permits

  • Look for steady foot traffic: markets, festival areas, bus stations.
  • Some cities require a temporary stall permit. Follow local rules.

3) Basic Setup

  • Foldable table/cart, portable light (for evening), simple signage.
  • QR/mobile payments, small cash float, reusable bags.

4) Pricing & Profit

Target a clear margin (e.g., $1–2 per item). Selling 50 items can net $50–100/night. Two nights weekly ≈ $400/month.

5) Attract Customers

  • Good lighting, clean layout, short promos (e.g., “buy 2 get 1”).
  • Friendly greeting matters more than fancy booths.

Summary

Start small, keep records, improve weekly. A simple stall can become a steady offline side income.
